Who Benefits Most from IV Therapy

This therapy is ideal for:

  • Athletes seeking faster recovery between training sessions
  • Professionals managing high-stress workloads and mental fatigue
  • Adults experiencing chronic low energy despite adequate sleep
  • Anyone recovering from illness or looking to boost immune function
  • Individuals with nutrient absorption issues (gut health concerns, post-surgery)

This may not be right for you if:

  • You have kidney disease or are on dialysis (requires physician clearance)
  • You're pregnant or breastfeeding (unless approved by your OB-GYN)
  • You have severe heart conditions without medical clearance
  • You're looking for a magic fix without lifestyle commitment

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an IV therapy session take?

Most IV infusions take 30-45 minutes. NAD+ therapy may take 2-4 hours depending on dosage.

Does IV therapy hurt?

You'll feel a small pinch during needle insertion, similar to a blood draw. Most patients find the experience comfortable and relaxing.

How often should I get IV therapy?

Frequency depends on your goals. Many patients start with weekly sessions, then move to bi-weekly or monthly maintenance. Our physicians provide personalized recommendations.

Is IV therapy covered by insurance?

Most wellness IV therapy is not covered by insurance as it's considered preventive care. We provide detailed receipts you can submit to your HSA/FSA.

What should I do to prepare?

Eat a light meal before your appointment and stay hydrated. Wear comfortable clothing that allows easy access to your arm.
